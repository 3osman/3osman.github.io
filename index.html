Efhamni

Efhamni (Understand Me) is a program for general gesture recognition, which is presented and tested with isolated signs of sign language. Other than common systems for sign language recognition, this framework makes use of Kinect, a depth camera developed by Microsoft and PrimeSense, which features easy extraction of important body parts. Recognition is done using hidden Markov models with a discrete observation probability. The program also offers an easy way of initializing and training new gestures or signs by performing them several times in front of the camera. Efhamni was awarded the second place award on a national competition (IBTIECAR 2012), sponsored by Microsoft ATL (Advanced Technology Lab) in Cairo.
We worked on this project as a team of five people, I was responsible for the system design, the research, and the Hidden Markov Model implementation.

FingerSense

During my job as a researcher in the Multimodal Cluster of Excellence in Germany, I worked on FingerSense. FingerSense is a system that can detect precise finger-to-finger interactive gestures using a depth sensor. The depth sensor of FingerSense can be mounted on the head or the shoulder, and it tracks mid-air gestures performed by the user. FingerSense can accurately track the hand, identify the fingers, and detect touch incidents between fingers. It can provide the exact touch location, defined by the touching fingers, and the exact touch point on the fingers. I investigated the novel design space of finger-to-finger interaction and designed a set of concrete gestures from this design space, utilizing the full dexterity and the 26 degrees of freedom of the hand. The prototype uses a depth sensor to enable the recognition of gestures based on finger-to-finger touches. The system evaluation obtained an 91% recognition rate of finger-to-finger hand gesture input from a database of 11 hand gesture classes collected from 10 participants

  • Date: September 2017
  • Field: HCI/Computer Vision

Video Annotation Using Eye Gaze Data

In this research project, we proposed a filtering approach that improved tracking accuracy for automated object trackers. The filtering approach used eye gaze data from viewers to label salient objects and objects of interest. The proposed approach can be further refined to work as an individual tracking algorithm, by just using eye gaze data from multiple viewers.
I worked on this project alone, under supervision of Hamed Tavakoli. We submitted a paper on this project to IPTA conference . Contact me privately if you want to check the paper
